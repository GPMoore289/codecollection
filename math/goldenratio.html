Golden Ratio Selection

Description: You're presented with 100 boxes, each containing a random amount of money. You may take the contents of one box only and you don't know what the upper limit of money is. You can examine the contents of as many boxes as you like, but you can't go back to a previous box. How many boxes should you inspect before making a decision? The maximum probability of taking home the largest winnings occurs if you inspect ~37 boxes, then take the next amount that is larger than any amount you have encountered previously. This only gives you a 37% chance of taking home the largest amount, but that's still your best chance, and this program demonstrates it. This has real-world applications, where there may be a time limit on making a decision with limited information, such as buying a house. This is another example of brute force computation and it takes a long time to run.

import random

cutoff = 0
while cutoff < 99:
    iterations = 0
    correct = 0
    while iterations < 100000:
        bank = list(random.randint(1, 1000000) for i in range(0, 100))
        highest = 0
        bnkidx = 0
        take = 0
        while bnkidx < 99:
            bnkidx +=1
            if bnkidx > cutoff and bank[bnkidx] > highest:
                take = bank[bnkidx]
                break
            elif bank[bnkidx] > highest:
                highest = bank[bnkidx]
                continue
            else:
                continue
        if take == max(bank):
            correct +=1
        iterations += 1
        continue
    print("Take after looking at " + str(cutoff +1) + " numbers: " + str(correct*100/iterations) + "%")
    cutoff += 1
    continue
