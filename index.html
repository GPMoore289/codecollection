This is a collection of my various coding projects since 2020. I generally write all code without use of AI, but have recently started to incorporate it into my work.

Projects labelled with the icon are the ones I consider to be the most advanced.

Games

This showcases my most advanced work: playable text-based games, some of which took several full days of coding.

Battleship: Find your opponent's ships and blow them up before they do the same to you.

Chess: My most complex project: a fully-functional two-player version of Chess.

Conway's Game of Life: Less of a game, more of a demonstration of complexity deriving from simple rules.

Final Fantasy VII Top Trumps: The classic two-player card game with FF7 characters.

Film Hangman: Guess a film's name before you run out of guesses.

Hearts: The classic trick-taking game played against 3 computer players.

Love Letter: Be the last one standing, or be the most prestigious one left standing, as you try to win the Princess' heart.

Mastermind: Guess a 4-digit code based on logic clues before you run out of guesses.

Noughts and Crosses: Known as Tic-Tac-Toe in North America.

Paper-Scissors-Rock: Every coder has to start somewhere...

Reversi: Also known as Othello, a battle to control the most spaces on the board.

Wordle: Guess a five-letter word based on logic clues before you run out of guesses.

Math Challenges

Sometimes, I'll come across a math problem during my internet perusal, and see if I can solve it using Python.

Birthday Calculator: Determines what day of the week you were born on.

Electoral College Calculator: Allocates Electoral College votes to US states based on population.

Fibonacci Calculator: Enter a number, program will print that many numbers into the Fibonacci sequence.

Frog Jump Problem: Determines average number of jumps a frog will make across 10 lilypads.

Golden Ratio Selection: How many items should you inspect before making a decision?

McNugget Problem Solver: Calculates the largest number of McNuggets you can't buy from McDonald's.

Prime Finder: Calculates if a number is prime, or its divisors if it isn't.

Utility Projects

A bit more of a mish-mash of programming challenges, but these are intended to have a more real-life (albeit niche) applications.

Binary Converters: Convert to and from binary.

Family Tree: Program that helps me navigate my partner's family tree!

Film Rankings: Short program that lets me quickly number a list of films in a text document.

Password Cracker: Demonstration of how brute force cracking is effective with weak passwords.

Temperature Converter: Convert Celsius to Fahrenheit and vice versa.

Game Solvers

Similar to the Math Challenges, but focused on solving game problems (mobile, video game or even board games) using code.

D&D Dice Roller: A simple way of solving a D&D dice-rolling conundrum.

Final Fantasy IX Skipping & Running: Two "bots" that automate Final Fantasy IX's two most awkward challenges.

Infinitode Bounty Solver: What's the optimal strategy for buying money-making towers in a tower defense game?

Missing Symbol Solver: Find the missing mathematical symbols to make an equation work.

Sudoku Solver: Solve multiple Sudoku puzzles during your lunch break.

Basic Programming Projects

These were created as part of the Programming Fundamentals, and Fundamentals of Unix courses at Mohawk College. I already had a good grip of Python by this point, so solving these problems were often trivial in most cases.
However, the course did introduce me to the use of AI in programming, and placed an emphasis on correct code-writing discipline, as well as identifying errors in code.
I was completely new to Unix, so the bash scripts are rather simplistic, but did net me some bonus marks.

Age & Gender: Output average ages for each gender based on CSV data.

Birthday Problem: Figure out the probability of X people sharing a birthday.

Change Finder: Figure out the minimum coins needed to make an amount of money.

Favourite Films: A very simple program for adding or removing items from a list.

FizzBuzz: The classic programming challenge - print Fizz, Buzz or FizzBuzz at the correct moment.

Inventory Manager: Add items, prices and quantities to a list.

Note Keeper: Store and retrieve notes from a stored file.

Palindrome Finder: Detects if a sentence is a palindrome.

Password Checker: Asks for password creation and accepts/rejects based on strict criteria.

Sentence Deconstructor: Summarizes all characters used in a sentence.

System Report: Generates a brief system report inside a Unix system.

Word List Filter: Add words to a list, then remove elements based on word length.

Trading Programs

I practised day trading for several months and incorporated Python into the data analysis, as well as writing Expert Advisors (automated trading programs) in a language based on C++.
The scripts here are niche and not easily accessible to the average reader, but I have included them for record purposes.
Using any of these programs to assist with day trading is done so at the user's own risk.

Converted Expert Advisor: An Expert Advisor that I converted from one language to another.

Forex Factory Web Scraper: Collects trading events from a forex information website.

Original Expert Advisor: An (unsuccessful) Expert Advisor I wrote from scratch.
